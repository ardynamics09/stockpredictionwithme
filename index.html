What is a Stock?

A stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a small portion of that company and become a shareholder. If the company grows, the value of your shares may also increase.

Why do people invest in stocks?

  • Grow money over long term
  • Earn dividends from companies
  • Beat inflation
  • Build wealth over time
  • Become partial owner of companies

Types of Stocks

  • Growth Stocks – Companies expected to grow rapidly.
  • Dividend Stocks – Companies that regularly distribute profits.
  • Blue Chip Stocks – Large stable companies like Apple or Microsoft.
  • Penny Stocks – Low priced stocks with higher risk.

Market Capitalization

Market capitalization refers to the total value of a company's outstanding shares. Companies are categorized based on their market size.

  • Large Cap – Large and stable companies with strong reputation.
  • Mid Cap – Medium sized companies with growth potential.
  • Small Cap – Smaller companies with higher growth potential but more risk.

What is an IPO?

IPO (Initial Public Offering) is the process through which a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time and becomes listed on a stock exchange.

After an IPO, investors can buy and sell shares of that company in the stock market.

Benefits of Investing

  • Long term wealth creation
  • Passive income through dividends
  • Ownership in successful companies
  • Portfolio diversification

Risks in Stock Market

  • Market volatility
  • Economic slowdown
  • Company performance issues
  • Global political or financial events

Important Stock Market Terms

  • Share – A single unit of ownership in a company.
  • Stock Exchange – Marketplace where stocks are traded.
  • Bull Market – Market condition where prices are rising.
  • Bear Market – Market condition where prices are falling.
  • Portfolio – Collection of investments owned by an investor.
  • Dividend – Portion of company profits paid to shareholders.
  • Volatility – Rate at which stock prices increase or decrease.
  • Market Trend – Overall direction of stock market movement.
